About Me

I am a Research Associate with a strong background in Cancer Biology and Genetics, currently based in New York City. My work focuses on understanding the tumor microenvironment of Glioblastoma (GBM), with a particular interest in the role of glioma-associated microglia and macrophages.

Through single-cell sequencing and other advanced techniques, I investigate the functional heterogeneity of immune cells within tumors and how they contribute to tumor progression and immune evasion. My goal is to identify new therapeutic targets to improve the prognosis for patients with this aggressive form of brain cancer.

Research Interests

  • Glioblastoma (GBM) tumor microenvironment
  • Single-cell heterogeneity of cancer and immune cells
  • Glioma-associated microglia and macrophages (GAMs)
  • Molecular mechanisms of immune evasion in brain tumors
  • Translational research for novel GBM therapies
